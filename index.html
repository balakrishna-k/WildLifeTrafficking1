The Illegal Wildlife Trade

Wildlife trafficking is pushing many species of animals and plants towards the brink of extinction. Reduced biodiversity as a result of this exploitation will lead to irrevocable changes to the food chain and the broader ecosystem. With overexploitation now becoming the second-largest direct threat to many species after habitat loss1, it is now imperative to understand why trafficking occurs, which species are most affected, and which nations are to be held responsible. This project aims to answer such questions and hopefully more.
